What is 3D-Printing

Also known as additive manufacturing, is the process of making three-dimensional objects from a digital file by adding successive layers of different kinds of material, until the full object is formed. We use a material called PLA, a type of plastic filament, in our prints.

Other materials you can print with are:

  • Metals
  • Carbon fiber
  • Graphene
  • Water-absorbing plastic
  • Stem cells

Other forms of 3D Printing

We use FDM printing to print our products, but there are lots of other methods of 3D-printing.The eight most common types used by hobbyists like ourselves are:

  • FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling)
  • Vat Polymerization
  • SLA (Stereolithography)
  • DLP (Digital Light Processing))
  • Powder Bed Fusion (Polymers)
  • SLS (Selective Laser Sintering)
  • MJ (Material Jetting)
  • DOD (Drop on Demand)

Digital Design

To 3D-print something, first you have to design it on the computer. Here are some common programs that are used to design prints.

Blender
This is the program we use. It is a free and open source 3D creation site. It supports the entirety of the 3D pipeline-modeling, rigging, animation, simulation, rendering, compositing, and motion tracking, even video and game creation.
Sketchup
A program where you start by drawing lines and shapes, the push and pull surfaces to create 3D forms.
SolidWorks
A solid modeling computer-aided design and computer-aided engineering program that runs on Windows.
Fusion 360
Another popular digital design program. Used commonly by hobbyist 3D-printers as well as professionals in engineering and manufacturing.
